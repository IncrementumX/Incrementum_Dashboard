## 1. Volatility ≠ Permanent Capital Loss

The fundamental distinction: price movement must be separated into signal and noise.
A 20% drawdown on a bad quarter may be noise. A thesis breach is signal. The job is
to tell them apart — not to react to every price move.

**A materialized risk is not necessarily a failure.** There are two distinct failure modes:

1. **(i) Misjudging the risk balance** — the risk was known but its probability or impact
   was weighted incorrectly. This is an analytical error, correctable with better process.

2. **(ii) Capital impairment from an unmapped risk** — a risk that was never identified,
   never stress-tested, never assigned probability. This is the graver failure. Missing an
   opportunity is never a mistake. Losing capital to a risk you never saw is always one.

**Tolerance to volatility is sizing-dependent.** A small position can be held through
drawdowns if the thesis is intact. A large position would trigger a stop and a rebuy —
not because the thesis changed, but because stops are a capital reallocation tool.

Example: position fell 20% post-earnings and recovered — held because it was small.
Had it been a large allocation, the stop would have triggered and capital redeployed.

**Risk control is thesis-driven first, price-driven second.**

## 2. Cash is an Active Weapon

Cash is not what remains after allocations — it is a position of conviction. Holding
cash when no high-conviction opportunity exists, or when macro warrants de-risking,
is operationally equivalent to the best short available (Druckenmiller framing, adopted).

Incrementum is an absolute return book. There is no benchmark to hug, no relative
performance pressure. Protecting capital in uncertain environments is the job.

**Demonstrated behavior (not stated preference):**
- Zeroed uranium before Iran war escalation
- Reduced gold/silver aggressively before war started
- Eliminated all portfolio leverage before the conflict began
- State as of 2026-04-27: holding cash + crude oil calls (tail hedge for escalation)

Pattern: Eduardo acts when the macro risk/reward degrades — before the market fully
prices it in. This is not prediction. It contains elements of macro read, intuition, and
luck; the goal is to systematize the read while preserving the flexibility to act early.

## 3. Sizing = Knowledge Proxy

Without conviction built on research, sizing up is not investing — it is gambling.

The rule: sizing is proportional to **depth of research**, not stated conviction alone.

| Position type | Knowledge level | Max sizing |
|---|---|---|
| Well-researched theme (metals) | Extensive | Large |
| Moonshot / growing conviction (BESI) | Building | Small-asymmetric |
| Interesting but under-researched | Partial | ≤5% |
| Watchlist (Bloom Energy, ASML) | Early | No position |

Missing an opportunity because conviction wasn't built yet is acceptable — and never
a mistake. Example: a position doubled without Eduardo having a large allocation because
the research wasn't deep enough yet. That is the correct outcome.

## 5. Tactical Flexibility (Shorts, Hedges, Options)

Eduardo operates with a full toolkit — but with strict conditions:

- **Shorts:** always with a defined stop (non-negotiable — upside is capped, downside
  is not). Opportunistic, not reflexive. Conviction must be built before entry — agents
  exist partly to support this work. Example: silver short while long gold when silver
  was overextended. Entry criterion is currently judgment-based — see Known Gaps (G3).
- **Options:** used for asymmetric tail scenarios (crude oil calls for Iran escalation).
- **Leverage:** used opportunistically; always eliminated before known risk events.
  Currently at zero.

### G1 — Correlation not actively managed in sizing
The book has high positive correlation in dollar-stress / risk-off scenarios (metals,
China, uranium, defense often move together). A correlation matrix exists but is
**monitored, not yet used actively in sizing decisions.** Critical before running leveraged.

### G2 — No portfolio-level loss limit beyond individual stops
Risk control is bottom-up: individual stop losses per position. There is no aggregate
drawdown threshold for the portfolio as a whole. If correlated positions gap through
stops simultaneously, total loss could exceed what any single stop protects against.

**Most critical operational gap before increasing gross exposure or running leveraged.**
