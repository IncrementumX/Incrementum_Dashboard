# Short Entry Criteria

> Synthesized from @incrementum-associate session, 2026-05-01.
> Primary audience: incrementum-analista and incrementum-associate agents.
> Status: v1 — approved by Eduardo on 2026-05-01.
> Resolves: Gap G3 (risk-first.md)

## 1. Core Principle

Shorts are opportunistic and tactical. They are not a structural allocation.
Every short has a defined stop before entry — non-negotiable.

## 2. Entry Criteria (Both Required)

1. **Positive risk asymmetry**: the expected downside in the underlying justifies
   the unlimited loss profile of a short position. This assessment may be driven
   by macro, technical, or fundamental factors — no single input is required.

2. **Ex-ante stop defined**: a specific stop level (price or condition) is set before
   the position is opened. The stop is not revised upward after entry.

## 3. Tactical vs. Thesis Shorts

Incrementum shorts to date have been tactical (hedge, correlation offset,
overextension in a correlated asset). A thesis-driven short — where the core case
is permanent impairment of the business — is a higher bar and would require
IncrementumOS research process before sizing beyond Seed.

## 4. Stop Discipline

Per risk-first.md §5: tactical shorts always carry a defined stop. If the stop is hit,
the position is closed. Re-entry requires a fresh entry decision, not a stop revision.

## 5. What This Does Not Cover

Sizing of shorts follows sizing-rules.md. Portfolio-level short exposure limits are
deferred to Tier 3 (levered book) per portfolio-risk-limits.md.
