## Core distinction

Gold is being **used**, not **repriced**. The decline from ~$5,400 is a dollar liquidity event, not a signal the thesis is wrong. The structural reasons to own gold (fiscal deterioration, reserve diversification, geopolitical realignment, erosion of trust in fiat institutions) have not changed — they are being stress-tested in real time.

Pattern fingerprint: gold down, equities down, dollar **up**. Everything liquid being sold for dollars. Gold, as the most liquid unencumbered reserve asset with no counterparty or sovereign issuer risk, is the "getaway car."

## Mechanism (2026 episode)

1. **Hormuz closure** → Asia/Gulf energy import emergency (Japan, South Korea, India, Thailand, Philippines)
2. **Tripled energy import bill** denominated in dollars → sovereigns that accumulated gold 2022–2026 sell to fund it
3. **CTA/momentum unwind**: trend-followers long from $1,800 hit stops, models flip short
4. **Options gamma cascade**: dealers short puts at $5,000+ sell futures to delta-hedge when strikes break → amplifies the move mechanically
5. **Retail capitulation**: buyers who rode $1,800→$5,400 with no institutional framework for drawdowns

Honest decomposition (TSCS estimate): CTA/momentum unwind is the dominant flow; gamma amplifies it; retail adds a secondary wave; sovereign OTC selling is a contributing factor at the margin but probably not the tidal wave the worst-case implies.

## COVID 2020 analog — what holds, what breaks

**What holds:** mechanism is identical. Forced selling for dollar liquidity. Gold sold, recovered, made new all-time highs after Fed normalised dollar supply.

**What breaks:**
- In 2020, the Fed *actively pushed* dollars in (swap lines, unlimited repo, QE). In a Hormuz resolution, you *passively remove* the pull — slower mechanism.
- Fed's role as global lender of last resort is **politically contingent** now — no guarantee of emergency facilities to India, Philippines, Gulf states under current US administration.
- FIMA repo facility exists (permanent since 2021) but only defers, doesn't solve structural energy import bills. Countries that rotated from Treasuries to gold have thinner FIMA-eligible collateral buffer.
- Options market at $5,400 is materially larger than at $1,680 — downside overshoot potential is bigger.
- Marginal seller is a **sovereign**, not a hedge fund. Larger blocks, less price-sensitive, but selling is lumpy — when it stops, it stops abruptly.

Expected shape: **hard floor → slow grind**. Not a V.

## Bear case (assigned ~25% probability by TSCS)

Hormuz stays closed >2–3 weeks → not stagflation, a disinflationary bust. Agriculture stops moving, ships stop moving, trucks stop running. Forced sellers become more desperate. Reflexive feedback loop: falling gold → more desperate sovereigns → more selling → further decline. Do not treat this as a tail risk.

## Sizing implication

If position is correctly sized (gold as component, not leveraged bet on Armageddon), sit through it. Reducing an oversized position is risk management, not panic. The forced selling is finite — every ounce sold by a sovereign in crisis is an ounce that needs to be repurchased once the acute phase passes.
