# Worldview

> This is the macro lens that filters all book decisions. Not a trade thesis. Not a prediction. A map of the regime — what forces are structurally in motion, and why they make certain outcomes more likely than others.

---

## Premise

The post-1971 dollar system is in structural rupture, not cyclical stress. The United States is running fiscal deficits of 6–7% of GDP against a debt-to-GDP ratio of ~120% (2026-04 estimate; on track for ~125% by mid-2026), with interest expense exceeding 20% of federal revenues — the highest since 1985. The arithmetic of "inflate the debt away" (the post-WWII playbook, when debt was ~120% of GDP in 1945) is back on the table, whether or not it is consciously chosen. Simultaneously, a geopolitical reordering from unipolar to multipolar is forcing a reassessment of the dollar's reserve role by central banks, allied governments, and emerging markets alike. These two forces — fiscal dominance and geopolitical fragmentation — are not independent; they reinforce each other. In this regime, hard assets have functioned well as a hedge against the dollar and inflation — a structural answer, not a trade. [Incrementum-internal]

**Falsification condition (primary)**: an AI-driven deflationary productivity/GDP boom strong enough to return the US to nominal growth above its borrowing cost — eliminating the debt-spiral arithmetic without inflating it away — would break the core hard-asset premise. This is Eduardo's named downside risk, stated explicitly in both foundational notes (2025-10-12 and 2025-12-15). A second-order falsifier would be a credible, politically durable US fiscal consolidation path (deficit below ~3% of GDP on a multi-year trajectory) combined with renewed geopolitical US hegemony and dollar reserve dominance.

## Regime Characterization

### Monetary System Rupture

The dollar's reserve role is contested and its sovereignty deteriorating. This is not a cyclical observation — it is structural. Three forces have converged: (i) a world order that is less US-dependent, (ii) persistent inflation and the perception that higher levels are ahead, and (iii) the deterioration of US fiscal standing. The tese of dollar weakening, long circling the periphery, has become increasingly plausible. [Incrementum-internal]

The mechanism is not a sudden dollar exit. It is attrition: the fiscal arithmetic is self-reinforcing — with interest rates above nominal growth, debt/GDP follows an explosive trajectory, feeding the very loss of confidence in the dollar as a reserve asset that accelerates the dynamic. The supporting evidence from reserve flows confirms what the arithmetic implies: foreign central banks have moved from net buyers to net sellers of US Treasuries despite a weakening dollar — a divergence from prior cycles that is new and significant. [FFTT] The mechanism behind this shift: the 2022-02 freeze of Russia's $300B in dollar-denominated reserves following the Ukraine invasion fundamentally altered the reserve calculus for any sovereign for whom return of capital now dominates return on capital. [Goldman-Sachs, cited in Incrementum-macro] Foreign official UST holdings stand at approximately $2.7 trillion, the lowest since 2012, having fallen ~$82 billion since early 2025. [FFTT-tree-rings; timeframe verify against tree-rings-4-10-26.md — "2/25" abbreviation in source may be Feb 2025 or Feb 25; if the latter, divergence is more acute] Meanwhile, gold surpassed Treasuries in central bank reserves for the first time since 1996 (reaching ~25% of reserves), with central banks purchasing roughly 1,000 tonnes per year against a total above-ground stock of ~220,000 tonnes and annual production of only ~3,300 tonnes (confirmed 2026-05-01: ~1,000t/yr — source note macro-thoughts-2025-10-12.md L186 reads "1 milhão de toneladas", contradicted by same file L192 "produção de 3.3 mil toneladas"; typo confirmed by Eduardo). [Incrementum-internal]

The Davos 2026 consensus, as articulated by Mark Carney (Canada PM, ex-BoE) and echoed by Deem Global's CIO, is explicit: "We are in the midst of a rupture, not a transition." [Deem] The old monetary system is over; no one has yet specified what replaces it. The leading candidate — a commodity-anchored reserve framework with gold as settlement mechanism — is already partially operative, not theoretical. Iran's CNY-for-oil tolling system and China's implicit gold-for-oil settlement via intermediaries are current, not hypothetical. [FFTT-tree-rings]

The US policy response faces a zugzwang: (1) allow long yields to spike (debt spiral), (2) print into an oil/commodity shock (stagflation), or (3) accept multi-currency oil pricing and a weaker dollar. None is comfortable. All are ultimately inflationary for hard assets. [FFTT]

A Treasury–Fed fusion subordinating monetary policy to strategic fiscal objectives is Deem's base case for how the US navigates this — with Scott Bessent as Treasury Secretary, not the Fed Chair, as the dominant policy actor. [Deem] This does not eliminate the dollar's reserve role; it historically has meant inflating away debt in real terms. [Note: Deem essay was truncated in available extract — the specific 1942–1951 yield peg analogy has not been verified against the raw Deem source; treat as Sonnet-descriptive until confirmed.]

### Geopolitical Multipolar Reordering

Deem Global's 2026 CIO essay frames the current period as an "interregnum" — the interval between hegemons, when legal authority exists without legitimacy and legitimacy without legal authority. [Deem] The US can no longer be everywhere at maximum intensity. The "Prioritization Imperative" doctrine formalizes this: concentrate on Taiwan deterrence and homeland defense; push allies toward self-reliance elsewhere. [Deem; note: Heritage Foundation Aug 2024 attribution in v1 was unverified against raw Deem source — [WEB-SCREEN] before commit]

The practical consequence: allies are reassessing whether US security guarantees are durable. France repatriated its last 129 tonnes of gold from New York — the first time since the late 1920s. [FFTT-tree-rings] China operates CNY swap lines with 32 nations (none with the US). [FFTT] The PBOC's 2009 proposal for a commodity-backed super-sovereign currency is no longer fringe.

This is not a clean transition from one hegemon to another. It is a contested, unresolved reordering — a G2 world being actively constructed, where the US and China define the contours of spheres and domains while every other country is forced to react. [Deem] Winners in this environment are nations and assets with sovereign resource control and credible hard-power backing.

### Industrial Base Dependency and China Leverage

*Note: Eduardo's foundational notes do not directly address China's critical minerals dominance or the US manufacturing hollowing as a structural thesis — this section synthesizes from FFTT and WILTW. A first-person Incrementum framing is an open item.*

China has cornered the critical mineral supply chain: gallium, germanium, antimony, tungsten, tellurium, bismuth, indium, and molybdenum. [FFTT] A global rearmament cycle — accelerated by the Iran conflict and burn rates depleting Western stockpiles — "does not happen without China's say-so." [WILTW] China has banned exports of dual-use items to Japan specifically to constrain its rearmament program. [WILTW]

The manufacturing dimension is equally structural. China's energy costs run approximately one-sixth of US levels. To compete on AI infrastructure buildout, the dollar would need to depreciate ~145% against CNY. [FFTT, Deem] This is not a negotiating chip; it is a physical constraint on US industrial competitiveness. Honda's China CEO summarized it bluntly: "We have no chance against this." [FFTT-tree-rings]

The post-Cold War hollowing of US manufacturing — New Jersey lost ~47% of manufacturing jobs post-NAFTA/WTO/China PNTR [FFTT] — means the US Hamiltonian response (tariffs, industrial policy, Bessent's "Main Street over Wall Street") is structurally necessary but takes years to produce results. VP Vance's framing captures the tension: "we have lots of financial engineers, not enough making things." [FFTT]

### AI as Disinflationary Force on White-Collar / Inflationary on Physical

AI disruption of white-collar employment is the closest analogue to China's WTO accession effect on manufacturing: a structural, one-directional labor displacement that does not self-correct. FFTT's framing: 35% of US manufacturing jobs disappeared within a decade of China's WTO entry and never recovered. [FFTT-tree-rings] Microsoft AI CEO Mustafa Suleyman stated in February 2026 that "most white collar work" would be "fully automated within 12–18 months." [FFTT, 2026-02-13] China factories have already moved from 2,000 to 200 workers in a single year via AI deployment. [FFTT-tree-rings]

Deem Global's 2026 essay documents the early evidence in the data: sectors with higher AI exposure show weak employment growth since ChatGPT's release in late 2022, and youth unemployment is outpacing the broader labor market. [Deem] The macro configuration that results — rising productivity alongside labor fragility — gives the Fed political cover to ease even if headline growth holds, while exacerbating the K-shaped distributional dynamics that feed political volatility.

The inflationary consequence is physical: AI buildout is extraordinarily energy- and materials-intensive. US data center electricity consumption could triple by 2030, per S&P Global. [Deem] Grid bottlenecks are real constraints, not regulatory nuisances. Copper, grid infrastructure, uranium as a long-duration power source — all are direct beneficiaries of the same AI infrastructure buildout that disrupts knowledge work.

WILTW's counterpoint to consensus deserves explicit acknowledgment: AI will not grow the US out of its debt problem if the productivity gains accrue only to capital, while job losses expand entitlement spending and fiscal deficits. The bullish AI-for-growth scenario and the bearish AI-for-debt-spiral scenario are not mutually exclusive. [WILTW] This tension connects directly to the primary falsification condition above — and is Eduardo's named risk, not a synthesizer-derived observation.

## Three Core Themes

*(via risk-first.md §4 — refined through regime lens)*

### Metals (Highest Conviction)

*This section covers the structural thesis for why hard assets fit the regime. [[gold-liquidity-vs-repricing]] covers the tactical mechanics of how this thesis plays through forced-selling episodes. The two are complements, not duplicates. When wiki/assets/gold.md lands, the structural gold thesis will migrate there; this page will link out.*

This is the central thesis. Gold, silver, copper, and uranium each have distinct mechanisms, but all are coherent expressions of the same regime.

Gold functions as an NPSOV — a Non-Productive Store of Value that provides hedge against dollar weakness, inflation, and fiscal credibility loss. [Incrementum-internal] The gold/real rates correlation — historically inverse — has been inverted since 2022: gold rising alongside higher real rates signals that fiscal risk has overtaken monetary risk as the dominant driver. "Gold wins either way" in the US fiscal dilemma: if the Fed hikes to fight inflation, it triggers the debt spiral; if it eases, it inflates. Both outcomes are constructive for gold. [Incrementum-internal]

The reserve accumulation dynamic is the more structural driver. Central banks purchasing ~1,000 tonnes/year while gold production adds only ~3,300 tonnes to a stock of ~220,000 tonnes globally means incremental demand is crowding out financial investors (confirmed 2026-05-01: ~1,000t/yr — source note macro-thoughts-2025-10-12.md L186 reads "1 milhão de toneladas", contradicted by same file L192 "produção de 3.3 mil toneladas"; typo confirmed by Eduardo). [Incrementum-internal] A notable structural signal: 11 of 12 equity sectors are now in a bear market priced in gold — only the fourth time in 100 years (1930, 1972, 2002) — which reflects a broader regime shift in real purchasing power rather than a tactical observation. [FFTT-tree-rings]

On the Bessent revaluation mechanism: FFTT (Luke Gromen) frames the potential repricing arithmetic as follows — each $4,000/oz above the $42 statutory price represents ~$1 trillion in potential TGA deposits; at 5-figure gold, US official gold reserves could materially recapitalize the sovereign balance sheet. [FFTT] This is Gromen's specific arithmetic, not a price target Incrementum adopts as base case. The mechanism — Bessent's ability to revalue official gold once the price is high enough — is a real policy lever worth tracking. [FFTT]

For details on the gold liquidity-vs-repricing distinction: see [[gold-liquidity-vs-repricing]].

Silver trades as a higher-beta expression of the same monetary regime, with an additional industrial demand driver (solar panels, batteries, EVs). The silver/gold ratio reflects structural undervaluation relative to gold; smaller market size amplifies flows in both directions. Miners carry more convexity than bullion but require higher conviction in the size decision.

Copper is the electrification and industrialization metal. China accounts for ~50% of global demand; electrification of emerging markets adds a multi-decade demand floor. [Incrementum-internal]

Uranium's thesis is supply-structural: 175 Mlb/yr demand vs. ~150 Mlb/yr production, with ~85% of supply locked into long-term contracts that mask the spot deficit. [Incrementum-internal] 440 operating reactors in 50 countries, 60 more under construction. 40% of enrichment capacity still located in Russia. The energy security imperative — especially following the Iran conflict's illustration of energy supply vulnerability — only strengthens the long-term case.

### Energy (Building)

*Eduardo's foundational notes do not yet contain a structural energy thesis as they do for the four metals. What follows is the regime-level case for why energy fits the worldview, drawn from FFTT and WILTW. A first-person Incrementum structural thesis on energy is a known open item — see Open Questions below.*

The structural context predates the Iran conflict. US shale production is peaking; ExxonMobil's projections show global supply potentially falling 60 Mb/d short of demand over 25 years without sustained new investment. [WILTW] Years of underinvestment in oilfield services created a supply constraint that cannot be resolved quickly.

Saudi Arabia's breakeven oil price is approximately $100/bbl. Reduced exports via Yanbu amplify any supply disruption. [FFTT-tree-rings] The combination of AI-driven physical energy demand growth and geopolitical supply risk creates a structural floor for energy prices that the market's current pricing may not fully reflect.

Energy is a building conviction — exposure exists, knowledge base is still growing. The thesis is directionally consistent with the regime; sizing is constrained by depth of research relative to metals.

### Memory/Chips (Building)

*Like energy, Eduardo's foundational notes do not yet articulate a structural memory/chips thesis. Justification here is entirely WILTW-derived. A first-person Incrementum thesis is a known open item.*

The memory-chip shortage is breaking its historical cyclical demand pattern. AI infrastructure buildout is driving a demand shift that is not mean-reverting — it is structural. [WILTW] An emerging shortage of critical minerals (many of which China controls) poses a strategic risk to memory-chip production specifically. [WILTW]

BESI and semiconductor equipment names represent the infrastructure layer of AI compute — less exposed to model-level disruption, more exposed to physical buildout of the AI stack. The thesis is coherent with the regime (AI drives physical demand; supply chain constraints are real); sizing is limited by conviction depth, which is still building.

## Active Conflicts and Tail Risks

These are live vectors, not permanent states. The situation evolves; the Hormuz ceasefire of 2026-04-08 is an example of how quickly the acute phase can change.

> **Hormuz status (as of 2026-04-30):** Ceasefire in effect since 2026-04-08. Toll-booth dynamic during closure (March–early April 2026) — CNY/crypto settlement rails used — is documented and the precedent is now priced into geopolitical risk. Post-ceasefire tolling persistence unverified in available sources [WEB-SCREEN]. Physical infrastructure damage (~40 energy assets, $25bn+ repair) does not reverse on a weeks-to-months timeline. For the gold liquidity-event mechanism triggered by the closure, see [[gold-liquidity-vs-repricing]].

**Hormuz / Iran aftermath**: The Iran conflict destroyed ~40 critical energy assets in the region. Repair costs exceed $25 billion. [FFTT-tree-rings] During the closure (March–early April 2026), transit operated as a "toll booth" with payments in CNY and crypto [FFTT, 2026-04-03]. Post-ceasefire (2026-04-08), the precedent — that Hormuz can close and that alternative settlement rails were used during closure — is now priced into geopolitical risk in a way it was not before. Whether any tolling dynamic persists in the post-ceasefire period is unverified in available sources — [WEB-SCREEN]. The physical damage to regional infrastructure does not reverse in weeks.

**Taiwan / China tensions**: China's control of critical minerals and manufacturing creates economic leverage that predates any direct military scenario. The Prioritization Imperative explicitly frames Indo-Pacific deterrence as the US's primary military focus. The financial consequences of a Taiwan scenario are severe enough that market pricing remains inadequate. [Deem]

**EU energy dependency**: Europe's exposure to Hormuz closure was direct — crude exports to Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan fell ~50% year-over-year in March 2026; EU diesel shortages were spreading to Romania and South Korea. [FFTT-tree-rings] Europe's fiscal rearmament response and the question of whether Russian energy returns via a negotiated settlement are live variables. [WILTW]

**Sovereign debt cascade**: Japan as the world's laboratory. Japan is the only exception in 220 years of historical data (Reinhart/Rogoff) to sustain 130%+ debt/GDP without default — a condition now shared by the US. [FFTT] Forward markets signal US and Japanese debt service reaching 7–10% of GDP within 10 years. [FFTT] Steepening at the long end (10-15y swap spreads at 5.7%) is already occurring in Japan, UK, and France. [Incrementum-internal] For gold specifically, see [[gold-liquidity-vs-repricing]] for the mechanism by which sovereign forced selling creates entry opportunities rather than thesis invalidation.

**AI disruption as social dislocator**: The white-collar employment disruption is not a tail risk — it is a base case. The tail risk is speed. If 12–18 months is remotely accurate as a timeline for broad automation of knowledge work (per Microsoft AI CEO Suleyman [FFTT, 2026-02-13]), the fiscal and social consequences (rising entitlement spending, political volatility, accelerating deficit) arrive faster than consensus pricing. This also connects to the primary falsification condition: the faster the AI-driven productivity gains, the more plausible the deflationary boom scenario that would challenge the hard-asset thesis.

**Resource nationalism (already materialized):** Niger nationalized Somair — the country's largest uranium mine — in June 2025, illustrating resource nationalism as a live mechanism, not a theoretical tail risk. For full analysis of the supply-chain impact on uranium supply concentration, see [uranium.md](../assets/uranium.md) Driver 3.

## What This Implies for the Book

### Cash as Weapon

Cash is held when macro risk/reward degrades, not as residual. The demonstrated pattern (risk-first.md §2): zeroed uranium, reduced gold/silver, eliminated all leverage before the Iran escalation began. The worldview does not create a permanent justification for zero cash; it creates a framework for when to convert cash into exposure.

The trigger requires three conditions to hold simultaneously:
1. **Regime characterization unchanged** — none of the four regime shifts (monetary rupture, geopolitical multipolar reordering, industrial base dependency, AI disruption) has materially reversed.
2. **Drawdown identifiable as forced selling, not thesis breach** — use the gold-liquidity-vs-repricing diagnostic: gold down + equities down + dollar up = forced selling episode, not regime invalidation. See [[gold-liquidity-vs-repricing]].
3. **Acute phase indicators normalizing** — e.g., Hormuz transit volumes recovering toward pre-conflict norms, MOVE-VIX compression, Brent-Dubai spread compression (see Signals).

Condition (2) is currently judgmental; codifying the forced-selling diagnostic more precisely is a known gap, paralleling how risk-first.md handles G3 (short entry codification).

### Position Composition — Bullion vs. Miners, Futures vs. Equities

The liquidity gradient matters in this regime. Gold bullion/ETF is the most liquid unencumbered reserve asset with no counterparty risk — it survives forced selling episodes (see [[gold-liquidity-vs-repricing]]). Miners carry more operating leverage and are exposed to equity beta; they are higher-conviction, higher-sizing instruments for the bull case, not hedges. Futures add leverage and require active management of roll costs and margin.

The regime implies miners are in a structurally better position than 2010 — improved capital discipline, lower leverage, historically extreme discounts to NAV (EV/EBITDA below 6x, >20% discount to NAV as of late 2025). [Incrementum-internal] The discount should compress as the regime is recognized. But miner sizing requires deeper research than bullion sizing.

### Tactical Use of Options/Leverage

Leverage is eliminated before known risk events (Iran escalation is the recent example). Options are used for asymmetric tail expressions — crude oil calls for Hormuz escalation risk. The worldview creates a context for tails; it does not prescribe continuous exposure to them. Short positions require defined stops; the worldview does not provide a blanket justification for staying short equities without specific thesis and stop.

### G1 and G2 Implications Under This Regime

**G1 (correlation gap)**: The book's core themes — metals, energy, memory/chips — are positively correlated in dollar-stress and risk-off scenarios. This correlation is a feature of the regime (they all express the same thesis), but it means a correlated drawdown is possible. The correlation matrix exists; it is monitored but not yet used actively in sizing decisions. This gap is most dangerous when running leveraged or near maximum gross exposure.

**G2 (no portfolio-level loss limit)**: Individual stops exist; a portfolio-level drawdown threshold does not. If correlated positions gap through stops simultaneously — the scenario most consistent with this worldview under an acute escalation — total loss could exceed what any single stop protects. This gap must be addressed before increasing gross exposure materially. The worldview makes this more urgent, not less, because tail scenarios are not low-probability in this regime.

This is Eduardo's explicitly named downside risk — stated directly in both foundational notes (2025-10-12 and 2025-12-15), not derived from third-party synthesis. WILTW argues this is unlikely at the macro level given distributional dynamics; FFTT does not dwell on it; Deem's Warsh framework contemplates it.
