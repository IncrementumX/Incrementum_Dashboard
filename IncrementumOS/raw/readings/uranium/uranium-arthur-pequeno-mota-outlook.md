CAMECO, BROOKFIELD, WESTINGHOUSE, US GOVERNMENT NUCLEAR STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIP
Cameco is up 14% in pre-market trading this evening after announcing that it has formed a strategic partnership
with the US government to accelerate the deployment of nuclear power in the US. As part of the partnership, at
least US$80b of new reactors will be constructed.
https://www.cameco.com/media/news/united-states-government-brookﬁeld-and-cameco-announce-
transformational-partnership

It is not clear how many new reactors will be built and who is funding them – but US$80b should probably fund at
least 10-12 new reactors, perhaps more if the economies of scale reduce the capex build, which is the point of
building out more than one at a time.

Each new reactor will consume about 0.5Mlb/yr of uranium – so today’s announcement is likely to increase US
uranium consumption by 5-6Mlb/yr next decade (from ~50Mlb/yr today). This is yet another increase in demand
for a commodity that is already structurally under-supplied. In our view uranium prices are going set new all times
in the coming super-cycle.

The announcement is the second signiﬁcant manifestation of Trump’s Nuclear Executive Orders after last month’s
announcement from the US DoE that it was partnering with 11 advanced nuclear technology companies to
accelerate the deployment of SMRs.

TRUMP’s NUCLEAR EXECUTIVE ORDERS
Trump’s four executive orders were:
Deploying Advanced Nuclear Reactor Technologies for National Security -
https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/05/deploying-advanced-nuclear-reactor-technologies-for-
national-security/
Ordering the Reform of the Nuclear Regulatory Commission - https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-
actions/2025/05/ordering-the-reform-of-the-nuclear-regulatory-commission/
Reforming Nuclear Reactor Testing at the Department of Energy - https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-
actions/2025/05/reforming-nuclear-reactor-testing-at-the-department-of-energy/
Reinvigorating the Nuclear Industrial Base - https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-
actions/2025/05/reinvigorating-the-nuclear-industrial-base/

The orders are aimed at accelerating the deployment of nuclear energy in the US, with objective of increasing US
nuclear energy output from 100GW to 400GW by 2050. As a rough rule of thumb, 1GW requires around 0.5Mlb of
uranium as fuel, so the US plans would add 150Mlb/year of uranium demand. When you also consider what China,
India, and every other developed nation (ex-Australia) is planning for nuclear power – it is pretty obvious that the
current global supply of uranium of ~150Mlb/yr needs to be dramatically expanded. That will not happen with
uranium prices below US$100/lb.

The orders are not just long term in nature and include immediate steps to boost nuclear output. The orders
include objectives to fund 5 GW of power uprates to the existing ﬂeet and ten new GW-scale reactors into
construction by 2030, support the restart of closed plants and ﬁnish half-built reactors.  Further, the Department of
Energy (DoE) will work with the Department of Defence (DoD) to examine the feasibility of restarting or
repurposing closed nuclear power plants as energy hubs for military micro-grid support. 

President Trump has long held an antagonistic view towards ‘independent regulators’, and the executive orders will
reform the Nuclear Regulatory Commission with the aim of speeding up the permitting process. Whilst this is
clearly an industry that needs a strong and independent regulator to ensure safe operations, the industry has long
been frustrated with the lengthy permitting processes that sometimes seem absurd. At the signing ceremony,
Constellation CEO Joe Dominguez gave the example of needing to prove to the NRC that a site is suitable for a
reactor build, when that site already has an operating nuclear reactor. There have only been two new reactors built
in the US since the 1970s, whereas China is building 10 each year. The orders will signiﬁcantly speed up the
approval process.

The backdrop here is the AI arms race with China. It is ironic that the announced success of China’s DeepSeek AI
engine caused uranium equities to sell-oﬀ earlier this year, when DeepSeek has in fact kicked of an arms race that
will cause huge demand for nuclear energy. Massive investment in data centres to drive the AI revolution needs
safe, reliable, environmentally friendly and always-on power. Nuclear is the perfect solution.

Where is the uranium going to come from?
In conjunction with the annual World Nuclear Association (WNA) Symposium in London, The WNA released its
2025 version of the biennial Fuel Market Report. Since the last edition in 2023 a lot has changed. Nuclear reactors
are being restarted, AI/data centre demand for energy has exploded, governments around the world (ex-Australia)
have realised that nuclear is a vital part of decarbonising power grids, insatiable Chinese energy consumption is
driving the fastest nuclear rollout in history, and the US has hit the nuclear accelerator to power its AI arms race
with China.

With the release of the 2025 Fuel Market Report the WNA is warning that availability of uranium could be a
limiting factor for the industry. In our view, there will be enough uranium – the key question is what is the required
incentive uranium price to develop the necessary mines. We think that number will be north of US$100/lb – and
could be a lot further north. Unlike most commodities, there is no ‘demand destruction’ price for uranium. It is not
substitutable, and it is a relatively small part of the cost of nuclear power (~5%). Utilities will end up paying what
they need to pay to ensure their reactor ﬂeets are fuelled.

The WNA has run three scenarios for nuclear energy capacity and hence uranium demand – a Reference Scenario,
an Upper Scenario and a Lower Scenario. The WNA estimates that there is currently 372 GWe of nuclear capacity
consuming 68,920t of uranium (~180Mlb of U3O8).

The Reference Scenario projects that nuclear capacity will reach 686 GWe by 2040 (up 60 GWe from the
2023 edition), which will consume just over 150,000 tU (~390Mlb of U3O8).
The Upper Scenario projects growth to 966 GWe consuming 204,000 tU (~530Mlb of U3O8)
The Lower Scenario projects growth to 582GWe consuming 107,000 tU (~278Mlb of U3O8)

The Reference Scenario predicts growth of ~120% in uranium demand to 2040. That is only 15 years away, and
with new uranium mines taking 10-15 years to bring on-line, it begs the question – ‘Where is this uranium going to
come from?’

In our uranium sector report from April 2025, we published our view of the supply and demand balance, our
projection had uranium demand reaching ~330Mlb by 2024, so well below the WNA reference scenario. However,
even with our lower demand outlook, we forecast massive supply deﬁcits by 2040 without signiﬁcant new
investment in capacity. The deﬁcit will be ﬁlled – but in our view it is likely to require uranium prices setting new all
time highs in the coming uranium super-cycle.

United States Government, Brookﬁeld and Cameco announce transformational partnership to deliver long-term

At least $80 billion to construct new Westinghouse nuclear power reactors
Partnership will accelerate nuclear power and artiﬁcial intelligence deployment in America

Tokyo & Cranberry, PA, October 28, 2025 – Westinghouse Electric Company (“Westinghouse”), Cameco
Corporation (“Cameco”) and Brookﬁeld Asset Management (“Brookﬁeld”) today announced that the United States
Government (“U.S. Government”) has entered into a strategic partnership to accelerate the deployment of nuclear
power, in accordance with the President’s May 23, 2025 Executive Orders.
At the center of the new strategic partnership, at least $80 billion of new reactors will be constructed across the
United States using Westinghouse nuclear reactor technology. These new reactors will reinvigorate the nuclear
power industrial base.

As a result of this historic agreement, nuclear energy deployment will be a central pillar of America’s program to
maintain global leadership in nuclear power development and Artiﬁcial Intelligence (“AI”).

technologies. This historic partnership supports our national security objectives and enhances our critical

A new partnership for growth
The partnership will facilitate the growth and future of the American nuclear power industry and the supporting
supply chain. Each two-unit Westinghouse AP1000 project creates or sustains 45,000 manufacturing and
engineering jobs in 43 states, and a national deployment will create more than 100,000 construction jobs. The
program will cement the United States as one of the world’s nuclear energy powerhouses and increase exports of
Westinghouse’s nuclear power generation technology globally.
As part of the partnership, at least $80 billion of nuclear reactors will be constructed using Westinghouse
technology. Once constructed, the reactors will generate reliable and secure power, including for signiﬁcant data
center and compute capacity that will drive America’s growth in AI.
The partnership contains proﬁt sharing mechanisms that provide for all parties, including the American people,
once certain thresholds are met, to participate in the long-term ﬁnancial and strategic value that will be created
within Westinghouse by the growth of nuclear energy and advancement of investment into AI capabilities in the
United States.
