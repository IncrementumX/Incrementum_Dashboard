planned. "There will be no single currency, but there will be a uniform payment platform," explained Russian Senator 
Vladimir Jabarov. The goal is a cross-border digital payment architecture that enables direct settlements in national 
currencies. 

Western countries are watching these developments closely. It is true that a functioning BRICS payment system 
would not immediately end the global role of the dollar. In the long term, however, it could limit the fiscal sanctioning 
power of the US and its European allies. Whether and in what form Brics Pay will be implemented remains to be 
seen. With the planned linking of central bank digital currencies, however, the project is taking on concrete 
institutional forms for the first time.

Anthropic, it is already substantially accelerating the rate of our progress in building the next generation of AI systems. This

I think the best way to get a handle on the risks of AI is to ask the following question: suppose a literal “country of geniuses” 
were to materialize somewhere in the world in ~2027. Imagine, say, 50 million people, all of whom are much more capable

Autonomy risks. What are the intentions and goals of this country? Is it hostile, or does it share our values? Could it militarily 
dominate the world through superior weapons, cyber operations, influence operations, or manufacturing? 

Misuse for destruction. Assume the new country is malleable and “follows instructions”—and thus is essentially a country of 
mercenaries. Could existing rogue actors who want to cause destruction (such as terrorists) use or manipulate some of the

Misuse for seizing power. What if the country was in fact built and controlled by an existing powerful actor, such as a dictator

existing balance of power? 

Economic disruption. If the new country is not a security threat in any of the ways listed in #1–3 above but simply 
participates peacefully in the global economy, could it still create severe risks simply by being so technologically advanced and 
effective that it disrupts the global economy, causing mass unemployment or radically concentrating wealth? 

Indirect effects. The world will change very quickly due to all the new technology and productivity that will be created by the 
new country. Could some of these changes be radically destabilizing?
