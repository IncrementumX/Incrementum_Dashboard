The reason the US is having so much difficulty re-opening the Strait of Hormuz was provided a year ago by 
former head of Blackwater Erik Prince, and it strongly suggests that the Strait of Hormuz may remain closed far 
longer than virtually anyone on Wall Street believes at the moment.

Here are the key excerpts of Prince’s February 2025 speech for purposes of this discussion (that we also highlighted in 
the 4/1/25 edition of FFTT, above): 

Prince called what happened in the Ukraine “the greatest advancement or swing of the pendulum [in warfare] 
since Genghis Khan put stirrups on horses”: 

They wanted me to talk about AI and the future of warfare. I think it's important to look back, to enable us to look forward and 
let's start where we are right now and what's happened in the Ukraine/Russia war has massively accelerated warfare in a way 
that I think it's the greatest advancement or it's the greatest swing in the pendulum, really, since Genghis Khan put stirrups on 
horses.  

Now, if you go back that far, I think that was 1218 or so, he set off literally the Mongol Empire when he was done, went from 
the Pacific to Hungary, and he mauled and terrorized his way through millions of millions of people and millions and millions of 
square miles of terrain. 

What stirrups on horses did is it allowed, instead of walking into battle or riding and having to get off and fight dismounted, it 
allowed people to stand up in the saddle, engage at a high rate of fire with a bow and arrow while riding forward and also 
riding backwards. And so now war so accelerated instead of moving at three to five miles an hour, now you could move at 20 
and 30 miles an hour.  

And the Mongol armies, of course, when he moved to the 150,000 people, soldiers, each of them had three horses. So you 
could ride hard, switch them out, ride hard again. And so he was moving a hundred plus miles a day and he just 
outmaneuvered and wrecked his way through societies. That's what's happened.  

Now, you haven't seen the full effects yet, but I'm telling you that level of change, a black swan event is possible because 
what's happened in Ukraine, when the Russian Army rolled in February of 2022 

… 

So what does this future of warfare look like as you look at even what's happening in Ukraine, first of all, don't listen to the idiot 
politicians that say, yeah, we've degraded the Russian Army. No, we have chewed up a lot of material. The Ukrainians have 
killed a lot of people unnecessarily. It's a really dumb war.  

The Russian army has gotten infinitely smarter. If you shot at a Russian with artillery in March or April of 2022, it would take 
'em an hour and a half to shoot back accurately. Now about two minutes, which means if you shoot at them, you better be in 
your vehicle and hauling a$$ because they're going to get you otherwise.
