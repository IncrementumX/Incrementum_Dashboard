# AI Is a 5-Layer Cake

**Author:** Jensen Huang
**Date:** March 10, 2026

## Introduction

AI represents foundational infrastructure comparable to electricity and the internet. It operates on physical hardware, real energy consumption, and sound economics — transforming raw materials into intelligence at scale. Both companies and nations will adopt and develop AI technologies.

## From Prerecorded Software to Real-Time Intelligence

Historically, computing relied on prestructured data and predetermined algorithms. SQL enabled this model by organizing and retrieving carefully arranged information.

AI fundamentally disrupts this approach. For the first time, computing systems can process unstructured information — images, text, and sound — while understanding context and intent. Critically, these systems "generate intelligence in real time" rather than retrieving prestored instructions, requiring an entirely reimagined computing stack.

## The Five-Layer Stack

When examined industrially, AI infrastructure resolves into five integrated layers:

### 1. Energy
The foundation layer. Real-time intelligence demands real-time power generation. Each token produced represents electrons moving, heat management, and energy conversion into computation. Energy functions as the binding constraint on system intelligence production capacity.

### 2. Chips
Processors specifically engineered to convert energy into large-scale computation efficiently. AI workloads demand substantial parallelism, high-bandwidth memory, and rapid interconnects. Progress here determines scaling speed and intelligence affordability.

### 3. Infrastructure
Systems orchestrating tens of thousands of processors into unified machines. This includes land, power delivery, cooling, networking, and construction — essentially "AI factories" manufacturing intelligence rather than storing information.

### 4. Models
AI systems understanding diverse domains: language, biology, chemistry, physics, finance, medicine, and physical phenomena. Language models represent only one category; transformative developments occur in protein AI, chemical analysis, physical simulation, robotics, and autonomous systems.

### 5. Applications
The layer where economic value materializes: drug discovery platforms, industrial robotics, legal assistance tools, and autonomous vehicles. Applications embody AI within machines and physical systems.

## Current Scale and Future Buildout

The infrastructure buildout represents "the largest infrastructure buildout in human history." Billions have been invested; trillions remain necessary. This expansion creates substantial demand for skilled trades — electricians, plumbers, network technicians, and operators.

Within the past year, models achieved practical utility at scale. Reasoning capabilities improved; hallucinations decreased; grounding enhanced substantially. Applications in drug discovery, logistics, customer service, software development, and manufacturing now demonstrate genuine product-market fit.

Open-source models prove critical, enabling researchers, startups, enterprises, and nations to participate. DeepSeek-R1 exemplified this impact — distributing advanced reasoning models accelerated adoption across all infrastructure layers.

## Implications

AI infrastructure fundamentally reshapes energy production, factory construction, work organization, and economic growth. Each layer reinforces others, creating compounding effects across industries, countries, and sectors.

The buildout remains early-stage. Much infrastructure doesn't yet exist; workforces require training; opportunities await realization.
